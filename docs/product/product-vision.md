# Product Vision — Beat Sketch

## 1. Product Overview

Beat Sketch is a personal music portfolio website built as an immersive, retro macOS-inspired desktop environment. It gives visitors a more engaging and memorable way to discover the creator's music compared to a standard portfolio, SoundCloud link, or list of embedded tracks.

The core problem it solves is that personal music portfolios are typically generic, static, and disconnected from the artist's identity. Platforms like SoundCloud, Bandcamp, YouTube, and basic portfolio sites are useful for distribution, but they strip away the visual world, mood, and personality behind the music. Beat Sketch turns music discovery into an interactive experience — visitors can explore folders, open windows, interact with visual elements, and play music through a custom retro-style player — making the portfolio itself a reflection of the artist's creative world.

## 2. Vision Statement

To create an interactive digital space where visitors can explore music as if they are entering the artist's personal retro computer world.

## 3. Target Audience

### Primary — Music listeners and curious visitors

People who discover the artist through social media, recommendations, search, or direct links. They need easy access to songs, a visually interesting experience, a sense of atmosphere, and a way to listen without leaving the site.

**Their needs:**
- Easy access to songs and projects
- A visually interesting experience
- A sense of atmosphere and personality
- A simple way to listen without needing to leave the website

### Secondary — Creative peers, collaborators, and potential clients

Musicians, producers, designers, and developers evaluating not just the music but the creator's overall artistic direction and technical execution. They need a clear impression of creative identity, a portfolio that stands out, and confidence that music, design, and development can be combined into one coherent vision.

**Their needs:**
- A clear impression of the creator's artistic identity
- A portfolio that stands out from standard websites
- A place to discover future creative work beyond music
- Confidence that the creator can combine music, design, and web development into one coherent experience

## 4. Key Features and Benefits

### 1. Retro macOS-inspired desktop interface

The website is presented as an old-school computer desktop, using visual elements such as folders, windows, icons, backgrounds, and system-like UI components.

**User value:** Visitors immediately get a unique first impression and feel like they are entering a designed world rather than just browsing a normal portfolio page.

### 2. Interactive music folders

Music is organized into folders, projects, beat tapes, demos, or categories. Visitors can open folders and explore tracks in a way that feels playful and familiar.

**User value:** Music discovery becomes more engaging and less passive. Visitors can browse the artist's work naturally, like exploring files on an old computer.

### 3. Custom retro music player

When a visitor opens or plays a track, a custom macOS-inspired player appears as the main listening interface.

**User value:** The listening experience feels integrated into the visual concept of the site, instead of relying on generic embedded players from external platforms.

### 4. Expandable creative archive

Although the first version focuses primarily on music, the structure can later support other creative content such as visuals, notes, samples, experiments, videos, or development projects.

**User value:** The website can grow over time into a broader personal creative archive without needing to change the core concept.

### 5. Strong personal branding

The combination of music, retro UI, custom visuals, and interactive browsing creates a distinctive identity for the artist — one that is entirely self-designed and self-built.

**User value:** Visitors are more likely to remember the site, share it, and associate the music with a specific visual and emotional atmosphere. The fact that the creator built it themselves signals fluency across music, design, and code.

## 5. Business Goals and KPIs

**Primary goal:** Create a memorable first impression that turns casual visitors into genuine fans.

### 3-Month Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Average session duration | 2+ minutes |
| Music play rate | 40%+ of visitors play at least one track |
| Bandcamp click-through | Measurable outbound traffic to Bandcamp |
| Organic sharing | Site shared or linked without prompting |
| Qualitative reactions | Visitors comment on the experience itself, not just the music |

**Future goal (out of scope for v1):** Support for selling beats, samples, or other creative work directly through the site.

## 6. Competitive Landscape

Without this product, visitors encounter the artist's music through SoundCloud, Bandcamp, YouTube, Instagram, or a basic portfolio page. These platforms are useful for distribution but strip away the visual world, mood, and personality behind the music — they present the work without the world it comes from.

The closest comparisons are interactive artist sites and creative developer portfolios, but these are rare and almost never self-built. The fact that this site is designed and developed by the artist directly is a meaningful part of the identity — it signals that the creator works fluidly across music, visual design, and code, and that every detail of the experience is intentional.

### What users do today without this product

| Platform | Strength | Limitation |
|----------|----------|------------|
| SoundCloud | Easy sharing | Generic, no personality |
| Bandcamp | Fan-friendly | Template-driven |
| YouTube | Wide reach | Passive consumption, no identity |
| Instagram | Discovery | Fragmented, algorithm-dependent |
| Basic portfolio | Full control | Static, forgettable |
