# Proto Persona — Kai, The Creative Peer

## Profile

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Kai |
| **Age** | 28 |
| **Location** | Remote or urban, active in online creative communities |
| **Occupation** | Musician and part-time developer or designer |
| **Music taste** | Jazz, lo-fi, hip-hop; drawn to producers who have a distinct sonic identity |

> "I can tell in the first ten seconds whether someone has taste or not."

---

## Context and Typical Day

Kai splits his time between making music and working on tech or design projects. He's deeply embedded in online creative communities — Discord servers, niche subreddits, music forums — where people share portfolios, beats, and personal projects. He regularly comes across links to other people's work and evaluates them quickly and instinctively.

When something catches his eye, he slows down. He's not just listening to the music — he's reading the whole presentation: the UI decisions, the visual choices, the level of craft. For him, how someone builds their portfolio says as much about them as the music itself.

---

## Needs

- A fast, clear signal of the creator's artistic identity and taste level
- Evidence that the creator can execute across multiple disciplines (music, design, code)
- A way to explore the work at his own pace without being funnelled into a sales funnel
- A contact point or path to reach out if he wants to collaborate

---

## Pain Points

- **Generic portfolios:** A basic site with embedded SoundCloud tracks signals low effort and kills interest immediately
- **Style without substance:** A site that looks interesting but has weak music is a red flag — the two need to be coherent
- **No clear identity:** If the aesthetic feels borrowed or inconsistent, he loses confidence in the creator's vision
- **Friction to connect:** If there's no obvious way to reach out after a positive visit, the moment passes

---

## Usage Behaviour

| | |
|---|---|
| **How he finds the site** | Shared in a creative community, social media, or a direct introduction |
| **Device** | Laptop — he evaluates portfolios properly, not on the go |
| **Visit frequency** | One-time or occasional; returns if the creator drops something new |
| **Session length** | 10–20 minutes if the site earns it — he goes deep when impressed |
| **What he does** | Explores the full desktop, listens to multiple tracks, reads between the lines of every design decision |

---

## Goals and Motivations

- **Evaluation:** Quickly determine whether this creator has the taste and craft to be worth collaborating with
- **Inspiration:** Discover a site that raises the bar and makes him think differently about his own portfolio
- **Connection:** Find someone who operates in the same aesthetic world — music, retro visuals, technical execution — and reach out
- **Validation:** See that a self-built portfolio can carry the weight of a serious creative identity, not just a polished agency site
