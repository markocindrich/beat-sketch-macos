> "I don't always know what I want to listen to — I just know when something feels right."

---

## Context and Typical Day

Sofia works a standard 9–5 and uses music mostly to decompress. Her listening happens late at night at home — headphones on, lights low, often while doing something else like browsing her phone or winding down before sleep. She doesn't actively search for new artists; she discovers them through links shared by friends, Instagram stories, or occasional social media posts.

She has an appreciation for retro aesthetics — vinyl, old-school visuals, lo-fi imagery — even if she can't always articulate why. When something feels intentional and atmospheric, it holds her attention longer.

---

## Needs

- A quick, frictionless way to start listening without friction or sign-up
- A visual atmosphere that matches the mood of the music
- Easy navigation to explore tracks without instructions or tutorials
- A way to support the artist if she connects with the music (Bandcamp)

---

## Pain Points

- **Mobile mismatch:** If she opens the link on her phone and the retro desktop aesthetic doesn't translate well, the experience immediately feels broken and she loses trust in the site
- **Generic feel:** A standard portfolio page or plain embed gives her no reason to stay — she'll close the tab and move on
- **Overwhelm:** Too many tracks with no clear entry point makes it hard to know where to start

## Goals and Motivations

- **Emotional:** Find music that matches her late-night mood without having to search hard
- **Discovery:** Feel like she stumbled onto something personal and rare, not a polished press kit
- **Connection:** Get a sense of who the artist actually is through the experience, not just the tracklist
- **Support:** If she connects with the music, follow through to Bandcamp and support the artist directly
